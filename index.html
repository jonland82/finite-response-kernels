One Kernel View

The common claim is that a terminal number is not enough. A limiting speed, variance, or exponent gives the endpoint. The kernel records how the response arrives there.

Mediated physical response

The observed field is the ideal source after it passes through a passive causal mediator.

\[ F_{\mathrm{actual}}(x,t)= \int_{\mathbb R^d}\int_{-\infty}^{t} G(x-x',t-s)F_{\mathrm{ideal}}(x',s)\,ds\,dx' \]
\[ K(t)\ge 0,\qquad K(t)=0\text{ for }t<0,\qquad \int_0^\infty K(t)\,dt=1 \]

Parallel channels mix. Sequential stages convolve. Hidden stages add spread rather than sharpening an already mediated response.

Recursive Takeoff

For recursion trees, finite response becomes the shape of redundant work before memoization identifies repeated subproblems.

Velocity takeoff kernel

A finite-lag recursion produces a tree-size sequence and a velocity profile:

\[ N_n=1+\sum_{j\in J}a_jN_{n-j},\qquad u_n=\log N_{n+1}-\log N_n \]
\[ F_n=\frac{u_n}{\alpha},\qquad \kappa_n=F_n-F_{n-1},\qquad F_n=(\kappa*H)_n \]

The profile \(F_n\) is the normalized step response. The kernel \(\kappa_n\) records immediate, delayed, oscillatory, mixed, or slow-settling redundancy onset.

Inverse problem

Inverse Takeoff Kernels

Turns the forward picture around. Given an observed takeoff profile, the draft asks which response modes can be recovered and which source mechanisms remain underdetermined. The organizing principle is that takeoff identifies response modes before it identifies source mechanisms.

A quick classical-motion twig: not one of the three main manuscripts, but close to the same finite-response intuition.

Smoothness Hierarchy in Classical Motion

This single-file note starts from time differentiation in classical motion. A jump in acceleration appears as an impulsive jerk; ruling out that impulse forces a smoother onset. The note then connects this regularity ladder to the transport geometry of mediation: ballistic propagation concentrates response on arrival surfaces, while diffusive mediation spreads response through Gaussian-like profiles.

\[ a(t)=a_-+\Delta a\,H(t-t_0), \qquad j(t)=\dot a(t)=\Delta a\,\delta(t-t_0) \]

Key Results

The papers repeatedly separate endpoint quantities from finite response geometry.

Passive composition preserves causality and adds spread

If \(K_1\) and \(K_2\) are passive causal temporal kernels, then \(K_2*K_1\) is passive causal, with additive mean and variance. The entropy-power refinement is strict for nontrivial causal densities:

\[ N(K_2*K_1)\ge N(K_1)+N(K_2),\qquad N(K)<\sigma^2(K) \]

The takeoff kernel is a real invariant

In the aperiodic finite-lag case, \(\kappa\) has total mass one, exponential settling, and modes controlled by the non-dominant roots of the recurrence denominator \(Q(z)\).

\[ Q(z)=1-\sum_{j\in J}a_jz^j,\qquad \text{mode}\sim(\rho/\zeta)^n \]

First-order overlap does not determine takeoff

Two schemas can share terminal tree speed \(\alpha=\log 2\), polynomial memoized state count, and limiting overlap velocity, while one reaches terminal redundancy immediately and another has an arbitrarily slow near-periodic transient.

Inverse takeoff is modal before mechanistic

Noiseless finite-exponential responses can be recovered by Hankel, Prony, matrix-pencil, or minimal-recurrence methods. But a branch-channel split \(a=b+c\) is not identifiable from one aggregate trace without added structure.

\[ \text{takeoff} \longrightarrow \text{modes} \longrightarrow \text{candidate mechanisms} \]
